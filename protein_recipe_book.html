Recipe 01 · Classic
Vanilla Protein Ice Cream
Xanthan gum + skim milk powder base
⌄
300kcal
38gprotein
22gcarbs
4.5gfat
Ingredients
  • Farm Fresh Skinny Milk400ml
  • ON Gold Standard French Vanilla Crème1 scoop
  • Skim milk powder1 tbsp
  • Xanthan gum¼ tsp
  • Vanilla extract½ tsp
  • Stevia powder (1:1 with sugar)1 tsp
  • SaltPinch
Method
  1. 1
    Blend everything
    Add all ingredients to blender. Blend 30–60 seconds until fully smooth and no powder clumps remain.
  2. 2
    Pour & freeze
    Pour into Ninja Creami pint up to max fill line. Level the top flat. Freeze at back of freezer 24 hours.
  3. 3
    Temper
    Remove from freezer and let sit 5 minutes before spinning.
  4. 4
    Spin
    Run Lite Ice Cream function. Re-spin once if crumbly or uneven.
Stevia note: Your stevia is 1:1 with sugar so 1 tsp = 1 tsp sugar sweetness. Taste before freezing and adjust — ON powder is already slightly sweet so this should be enough.
Recipe 02 · Chocolate
Chocolate Protein Ice Cream
Deep cocoa, fudgy and dense at 300ml
⌄
295kcal
38gprotein
21gcarbs
4.5gfat
Ingredients
  • Farm Fresh Skinny Milk300ml
  • ON Gold Standard French Vanilla Crème1 scoop
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder2 tbsp
  • Skim milk powder1 tbsp
  • Xanthan gum¼ tsp
  • Stevia powder (1:1 with sugar)1½ tsp
  • SaltPinch
Method
  1. 1
    Dissolve cocoa first
    Warm 50ml of milk in microwave 20 seconds. Whisk in cocoa powder and stevia until fully dissolved — no lumps.
  2. 2
    Blend everything
    Add cocoa mixture to blender with remaining milk, protein powder, skim milk powder, xanthan gum, and salt. Blend 30–60 seconds.
  3. 3
    Taste & adjust
    Cocoa is bitter so needs more stevia than vanilla — 1½ tsp is the starting point. Adjust before freezing.
  4. 4
    Pour & freeze
    Pour into pint, level top flat, freeze 24 hours at back of freezer.
  5. 5
    Temper & spin
    Rest 5 mins, run Lite Ice Cream, re-spin once if needed.
Tip: Dissolving cocoa in warm milk first prevents bitter gritty lumps. Dutch-process cocoa gives a deeper flavour — worth looking for at Cold Storage.
Recipe 03 · Fruity
Strawberry Cheesecake
Tangy, fruity, naturally thickened by strawberry pectin
⌄
292kcal
36gprotein
22gcarbs
3gfat
Ingredients
  • Farm Fresh Skinny Milk250ml
  • ON Gold Standard French Vanilla Crème1 scoop
  • Fresh strawberries, hulled80g
  • Fat free cream cheese1 tbsp
  • Skim milk powder1 tbsp
  • Xanthan gum¼ tsp
  • Lemon juice½ tsp
  • Stevia powder (1:1 with sugar)1 tsp
  • SaltPinch
Method
  1. 1
    Blend strawberries first
    Blend strawberries alone until completely smooth — no chunks or they freeze into icy bits.
  2. 2
    Add everything & blend
    Add milk, protein powder, cream cheese, skim milk powder, xanthan gum, lemon juice, stevia, and salt. Blend 30–60 seconds.
  3. 3
    Taste & adjust
    Should taste slightly sweeter and more tangy than you want — flavour mutes when frozen. Lemon is reduced to ½ tsp to avoid sourness overpowering.
  4. 4
    Pour & freeze
    Pour into pint, check it hits the min fill line, level top, freeze 24 hours.
  5. 5
    Temper & spin
    Rest 5 mins, run Lite Ice Cream, re-spin once if needed.
Fix for sourness: Lemon juice reduced from 1 tsp to ½ tsp and stevia increased to 1 tsp to balance the natural acidity of strawberries. If still too tart, reduce lemon further or skip it entirely.

Recipe 01 · Fruity
Frozen Strawberry Ice Cream
Light, fruity — strawberries added after spinning
⌄
194kcal
14gprotein
26gcarbs
1gfat
Ingredients
  • Farm Fresh Skinny Milk300ml
  • Frozen strawberries (for mix-in after spinning)80g
  • Fat free cream cheese1 tbsp
  • Skim milk powder1 tbsp
  • Xanthan gum¼ tsp
  • Stevia powder (1:1 with sugar)1 tsp
  • Vanilla extract½ tsp
  • SaltPinch
Method
  1. 1
    Make the base — no strawberries yet
    Blend milk, cream cheese, skim milk powder, xanthan gum, stevia, vanilla extract, and salt until smooth. Do NOT add strawberries at this stage.
  2. 2
    Pour & freeze
    Pour plain base into Ninja Creami pint up to max fill line. Level top flat. Freeze at back of freezer 24 hours.
  3. 3
    Temper
    Remove from freezer and let sit 5 minutes on the counter.
  4. 4
    Spin the base first
    Run Lite Ice Cream function on the plain base. Let the full cycle complete.
  5. 5
    Add strawberries & use Mix-In
    Lightly thaw the frozen strawberries — just enough to be pliable but still cold. Make a well in the centre of the spun ice cream. Add strawberries and run the Mix-In function. This gives chunky real strawberry pieces throughout.
  6. 6
    Serve immediately
    Serve straight from the pint. The strawberry chunks will be slightly icy and chewy — that contrast is what makes this recipe.
Why add strawberries after? Adding post-spin gives you distinct chunky fruit pieces instead of a uniformly pink base. The contrast of creamy base and slightly icy chunks is the best part of this recipe.
Recipe 02 · Milo
Milo Ice Cream
Classic Milo taste — nostalgic and low cal
⌄
215kcal
13gprotein
31gcarbs
2gfat
Ingredients
  • Farm Fresh Skinny Milk300ml
  • Milo powder3 tbsp
  • Fat free cream cheese1 tbsp
  • Skim milk powder1 tbsp
  • Xanthan gum¼ tsp
  • Stevia powder (1:1 with sugar)1½ tsp
  • Vanilla extract1 tsp
  • SaltPinch
Method
  1. 1
    Dissolve Milo first
    Warm 50ml of milk in microwave 20 seconds. Add Milo powder and stir until fully dissolved — Milo leaves gritty lumps if mixed in cold milk.
  2. 2
    Blend everything
    Add Milo mixture to blender with remaining milk, cream cheese, skim milk powder, xanthan gum, stevia, vanilla, and salt. Blend 30–60 seconds until smooth.
  3. 3
    Taste & adjust
    Milo already has sugar but freezing mutes it significantly. The 1½ tsp stevia and 3 tbsp Milo should give a strong enough flavour — taste and adjust if needed.
  4. 4
    Pour & freeze
    Pour into Ninja Creami pint, level top flat, freeze 24 hours at back of freezer.
  5. 5
    Temper & spin
    Rest 5 mins on counter, run Lite Ice Cream, re-spin once if crumbly.
  6. 6
    Optional — Milo Dinosaur topping
    Sprinkle dry Milo powder on top after spinning for that classic Milo Dinosaur look.
Fix for blandness: Bumped Milo from 2 to 3 tbsp and stevia from ½ to 1½ tsp. Milo flavour fades significantly when frozen so you need more than you think.
Recipe 03 · Durian
Durian Ice Cream
Naturally creamy from durian fat — no cream cheese needed
⌄
254kcal
8gprotein
35gcarbs
5gfat
Ingredients
  • Fresh durian flesh (seeds removed)100g
  • Farm Fresh Skinny Milk200ml
  • Skim milk powder1 tbsp
  • Xanthan gum¼ tsp
  • Stevia powder (1:1 with sugar)¾ tsp
  • SaltPinch
Method
  1. 1
    Blend durian first
    Blend durian flesh alone until completely smooth. No seeds, no fibrous bits — strain if needed.
  2. 2
    Add everything & blend
    Add milk, skim milk powder, xanthan gum, stevia, and salt to the blender. Blend 30–60 seconds until silky smooth.
  3. 3
    Taste & adjust
    Durian is naturally sweet and pungent — taste before freezing. ¾ tsp stevia should be enough but adjust if needed. Go slightly sweeter than you want since freezing mutes flavour.
  4. 4
    Pour & freeze
    Pour into Ninja Creami pint, level top flat, freeze 24 hours at back of freezer.
  5. 5
    Temper & spin
    Rest 5 mins on counter. Run Lite Ice Cream. Re-spin once if needed.
Why no cream cheese? Durian has natural fat (~5g per 100g) which acts as a built-in emulsifier and creaminess booster. This will be your smoothest spinning low cal recipe. Milk is reduced to 200ml to account for the volume and moisture from the durian flesh itself.

Air Fried Fillet & Rice
Classic — 3 seasoning options
⌄
400kcal
40gprotein
50gcarbs
6gfat
Ingredients
  • Inner fillet (beef tenderloin)150g
  • White rice (cooked)150g
  • Salad or cucumberSide
Seasoning Options
  • Option A — SimpleSalt, pepper, garlic powder
  • Option B — Soy garlicSoy sauce + minced garlic + pepper
  • Option C — SpicedCumin + paprika + garlic powder + salt
Method
  1. 1
    Season the fillet
    Pat dry with paper towel — moisture stops it browning. Coat with your chosen seasoning on all sides.
  2. 2
    Air fry
    Air fry at 200°C for 10–12 minutes, flipping halfway. Rest 3 minutes before cutting.
  3. 3
    Reheat rice
    Cook rice night before. Reheat in microwave 90 seconds with a splash of water.
  4. 4
    Serve
    Plate fillet, rice, and salad.
Meal prep tip: Air fry 3–4 fillets at once. Store in fridge up to 3 days. Reheat in air fryer 3 mins at 180°C.

36gcarbs
6gfat
Ingredients
  • Inner fillet150g
  • Russet potato~200g
  • Mixed saladSide
  • Cumin, paprika, garlic powder, saltTo taste
Method
  1. 1
    Prep potato wedges
    Cut into wedges. Season with paprika, garlic powder, salt. No oil needed in air fryer.
  2. 2
    Air fry potato
    Air fry at 200°C for 18–20 minutes, shaking halfway. Crispy outside, soft inside.

    Serve
    Plate everything together.
Tip: Put wedges in first for 8 minutes, then add fillet for the remaining 12 minutes — both done at the same time.

Fillet, Sweet Potato & Veg
Light, filling, high protein — the perfect cut dinner
⌄
370kcal
40gprotein
28gcarbs
6gfat
Ingredients
  • Inner fillet150g
  • Sweet potato200g
  • Broccoli, bok choy, or any veg200g
  • Soy sauce or oyster sauce1 tbsp
  • Garlic powder, salt, pepperTo taste
Method
  1. 1
    Prep sweet potato
    Cut into cubes or wedges. Season lightly with salt. Air fry at 200°C for 16–18 minutes until caramelised outside, soft inside.
  2. 2
    Steam the veg
    Steam or microwave veg 4–5 minutes until just tender. Season with soy or oyster sauce.
  3. 3
    Air fry fillet
    Season with garlic powder, salt, pepper. Air fry at 200°C for 10–12 minutes flipping halfway. Rest 3 minutes before slicing.
  4. 4
    Serve
    Plate everything. Swap sweet potato for regular potato or rice if you want more carbs.
Tip: Sweet potato keeps you fuller longer — great choice before bed so you don't feel hungry overnight.

Ninja Creami Tips

Everything you need for a perfect spin every time

🧊
Always use Lite Ice Cream

Low fat bases respond better to Lite Ice Cream, not the regular function.

⏱️
Temper 5 minutes

Let the pint sit 5 minutes before spinning. Slightly thawed = noticeably creamier result.

📐
Flat top when freezing

No dome on the surface. A flat top means the blade spins evenly across the whole pint.

❄️
Back of the freezer

Place pints at the back, not the door. More consistent temp = better freeze = better spin.

🔄
Re-spin is your friend

If the result looks crumbly after the first spin, hit Re-spin once. Usually all it takes.

🍫
Dissolve powders in warm milk

Always warm a small amount of milk and dissolve cocoa or Milo first. Prevents gritty lumps.

🧪
Taste before freezing

Flavour mutes when frozen. Base should taste slightly sweeter than you want the final result.

📅
Prep the night before

Needs 24 hours to freeze solid. Prep tomorrow's pint right after eating today's.

Reference
The Low Fat Creami Stack
How to get creamy results without the fat
  • Xanthan gum (¼ tsp) — prevents large ice crystals forming~0 kcal
  • Skim milk powder (1 tbsp) — reduces free water in the base~15 kcal
  • Fat free cream cheese (1 tbsp) — emulsifies, adds body~15 kcal
  • Stevia powder (1:1) — adjust to taste, always taste before freezing~0 kcal
Stevia guide (1:1 ratio): Vanilla = 1 tsp · Chocolate = 1½ tsp · Strawberry cheesecake = 1 tsp · Milo = 1½ tsp · Durian = ¾ tsp (naturally sweet). Always taste before freezing — frozen things taste less sweet so the base should taste slightly sweeter than your target.
